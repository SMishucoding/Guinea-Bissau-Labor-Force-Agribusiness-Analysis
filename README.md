# Guinea-Bissau-Labor-Force-Agribusiness-Analysis
# 🇬🇼 Guinea-Bissau Labor Force & Agribusiness Analysis

## 📊 Understanding Employment, Productivity, and Structural Transformation

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of labor market dynamics in Guinea-Bissau, with a focus on the transition from primary agriculture to agribusiness. Using nationally representative Labor Force Survey (LFS) data, the analysis examines workforce composition, job quality, and structural constraints affecting economic transformation.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Distinguish between primary agriculture and agribusiness employment  
- Assess job quality across sectors (formality, occupation, earnings)  
- Analyze workforce composition by gender, age, and education  
- Identify who accesses higher-value agribusiness jobs  
- Highlight structural constraints limiting labor mobility  

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

### 1. Agriculture Dominates Employment
The majority of workers are concentrated in primary agriculture, while agribusiness remains small and underdeveloped.

### 2. Extremely Low Levels of Education
Approximately 94% of workers have no or only primary education, limiting access to higher-productivity employment.

### 3. Agribusiness Requires Higher Human Capital
Workers in agribusiness are relatively more educated, but even educated individuals remain largely in agriculture, indicating limited job absorption.

### 4. Informality is Systemic
- Agriculture: ~95–99% informal  
- Agribusiness: ~80–90% informal  

Informality is a structural feature across the entire labor market.

### 5. Gender Dynamics Are Complex
Women are more educated than men in this workforce but remain concentrated in informal and lower-value roles.

### 6. Youth Are Concentrated in Agriculture
Younger workers are heavily concentrated in agriculture, while agribusiness is dominated by prime working-age individuals (25–44).

### 7. Earnings Remain Low
Most workers fall into low income categories, with limited differentiation across sectors.

## 📈 Methodology

### Data Source
- National Labor Force Survey (LFS)

### Sector Classification

| Category | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| Primary Agriculture | Crop, livestock, fisheries |
| Agribusiness | Processing, distribution, services, inputs |

### Key Variables

- sector_group: Agriculture vs Agribusiness  
- education_group: No education to Tertiary  
- gender: Male / Female  
- age_group: 15–24 to 55+  
- formal: Composite formality indicator  
- TP23A_RS: Earnings category (ordinal)

---

## 🚧 Constraints Identified

### Supply-Side Constraints
- Low education levels  
- Limited mobility out of agriculture  
- Gender-related structural barriers  

### Demand-Side Constraints
- Limited job creation in agribusiness  
- Weak value chains  
- Skills mismatch  
- High informality  

---

## 🌍 Policy Implications

- Invest in education and skills development  
- Strengthen agribusiness value chains  
- Support firm growth and formalization  
- Enable youth transition into higher-value sectors  
- Address gender-specific labor market barriers
